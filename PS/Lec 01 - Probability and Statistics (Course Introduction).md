| **Final Examination** | **60 Marks** |

## 3. Core Course Content

The syllabus covers foundational to advanced statistical modeling techniques highly applicable in computing and data science:
1. Introduction to Statistics 2. Probability Distributions 3. Inferential Statistics 4. Regression Analysis 5. Time Series Analysis and Prediction 6. Queueing Theory > [!NOTE]
> **Bloom's Taxonomy Focus:**
> The course aims to develop cognitive skills across all levels of Bloom's Taxonomy, progressing from basic recall (Remembering/Understanding) to advanced application and synthesis (Analyzing, Evaluating, and Creating/Designing original work) .

## 4. Recommended References

To support your learning across the different modules, the following textbooks are recommended:

* **Probability & General Statistics:** * *Introduction to Probability and Statistics for Engineers and Scientists (5th Edition)* by S.M. Ross (Elsevier Science Publishing) .
* **Time Series Module:** * *Introductory Time Series with R (1st Edition)* by P. S. P. Cowpertwait and A. V. Metcalfe (Springer) .
* **Queueing Theory Module:** * *Fundamentals of Queueing Theory (1st Edition)* by J. F. Shortle, J. M. Thompson, D. Gross, C.M. Harris (John Wiley Sons, Inc.) .
